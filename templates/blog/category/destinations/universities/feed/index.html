Data Analytics and Data Science

Data Analytics and Data Science are two sides of the data analysis coin but with distinct purposes. Data Analytics professionals are the detectives of the data world, meticulously examining past data through statistical methods and visualization tools. They explore trends, patterns, and relationships within this historical data to inform business decisions and solve current problems. They often work with structured data, which is neatly organized in databases and spreadsheets. On the other hand, data scientists are futurists in data analysis. They analyze historical data and incorporate real-time information. Their toolbox is more extensive, including machine learning and artificial intelligence, which allows them to build models that predict future outcomes. They might explore unstructured data like text, images, and social media feeds to uncover hidden patterns and develop innovative solutions for complex challenges. In simpler terms, data analytics is about understanding the past and making informed decisions based on that knowledge. On the other hand, data science is about predicting the future and using data to solve complex problems These emerging fields experiencing high demand, so the program you choose depends on your career goals. Do you want to be a master of historical data analysis or a pioneer who uses data to predict the future? The universities and colleges in Canada are different institutions. Colleges have various programs than universities do. If you plan to study in Canadian colleges, first you should know the differences between these institutions and universities.

What are Colleges in Canada?

Colleges of applied arts and technology have a full-time and part-time diploma and certificate programs. Many also offer Bachelor’s degrees in applied areas of study.

The QS Global MBA Rankings is a business school ranking from around the world. This ranking comes from employability, entrepreneurship and alumni outcomes, return on investment, thought leadership, and diversity.

Based on the QS Global MBA Rankings, this article provides the top 10 MBA programs in Europe in 2021. The top business schools in Europe come as following.

 

1. HEC Paris, France

  • Tuition fees: US $76434
  • Percentage of international students: 95

HEC is one of the most respected business schools, located in Jouy-en-Josas, France.

HEC Paris earned first place in Europe for yet another year in the MBA programs, and also achieved the highest score in the European rankings for ROI and scored very highly for thought leadership and employability.

HEC graduates have been known to use a massive 152 percent salary increase post-MBA, with students earning a large average salary of US $128206.

The 15-month program concentrates on developing management potentials, such as leadership seminars, an MBA tournament, MBA multisport, and the HEC Global Leaders Series, with famous speakers from the business world.

 

2. INSEAD, Top Business Schools in Europe

  • Tuition fees: US $98009
  • Percentage of international students: 91

INSEAD is another business school located worldwide, including Europe, Asia, and North America.

This program has the second rank in Europe, and sixth in the world is the MBA program of this institution.

INSEAD MBA program scored the second-highest spot for employability and the highest for thought leadership in Europe.

Ninety-two percent of students accepted job offers three months after graduation, noting an average salary of US $105900.

During the 10-month program, students can study at INSEAD’s three campuses in Fontainebleau, Abu Dhabi, and Singapore. They complete 14 core courses and have a choice from over 75 elective options.

3. London Business School, England

  • Tuition fees: US $104493
  • Percentage of international students: 92

London Business School (LBS) is a business school and a constituent college of the federal University of London.

Landing the leading British MBA program in Europe for yet another year is London Business School’s 21-month MBA program, which earned the first spot for employability in the top 10 of Europe.

London is one of the world’s best financial capitals, with 100 percent of the world’s leading financial institutions represented in the city and three-quarters of the top 500 worldwide companies using it as their base.

Their employment rates are impressive, with 92 percent of MBA graduates accepting a job offer three months after their graduation, with average salaries of US $106837.

 

4. IE Business School, Spain

  • Tuition fees: US $79634
  • Percentage of international students: 91

IE Business School of Madrid is a graduate and undergraduate school of business. It offers its 11-month International MBA program, which incidentally earned the top spot in the European top 10 for Entrepreneurship and Alumni outcomes.

The program also made the top spot for variety in Europe. International students worldwide go there as one of the top business schools in Europe in 2021.

IE’s MBA has the Professional Fitness program on which all MBA students are enrolled, an exciting addition. This program highlights two distinct elements: Career Fitness and Behavioral Fitness.

Career Fitness helps you land your dream job through advice and workshops, whereas Behavioral Fitness practices more productive workplace behaviors and improves leadership skills.

 

5. IESE Business School, Spain

  • Tuition fees: US $93592
  • Percentage of international students: 90

Barcelona’s IESE Business School is an international business school offering MBA and Executive Education programs; it is one of its best since 2015.

This business school offers MBA students the opportunity to complete its program between 15 and 19 months.

The MBA program scored second-highest in the top 10 of Europe for ROI, with an impressive average post-graduation salary uplift of 124 percent, earning an average salary of $123662 three months after graduation.

Ninety-four percent of students accepted jobs three months after graduation, and 10 percent started their own business.

The school supports connection with its Spanish roots, giving a Business Spanish learning program for MBA students and the option for advanced and native Spanish speakers to graduate with a bilingual MBA diploma.

 

6. ESADE, Top Business Schools in Europe

  • Tuition fees: US $76830
  • Percentage of international students: 95

ESADE is a private Jesuit educational institution within the Ramon Llull University in Barcelona, Spain.

Students undertaking ESADE’s 18-month MBA program enjoy studying the third best program in the European top 10 for ROI.

ESADE’s MBA program has a tremendous international focus. Study tours take students to a range of destinations on five different continents.

International students can also do a global exchange of up to four months from a choice of 45 partner institutions, including Dartmouth College Tuck School of Business, Warwick Business School, and China Europe International Business School (CEIBS).

The program is mainly in English; however, Spanish, French, and German lessons are available too.

 

7. Saïd Business School, England

  • Tuition fees: US $80430
  • Percentage of international students: 95

Saïd Business School is a great business school at the University of Oxford. It is named after Wafic Saïd and is part of Oxford’s Social Sciences Division.

This business school is the business School of Oxford University, the oldest university in the English-speaking world.

The one-year MBA program at Saïd institution follows the more comprehensive university’s three-term structure, divided into sets of core and elective modules concentrating on three themes related to the world business challenges: Entrepreneurship, Responsible Business, and Global Rules of the Game.

Ninety-one percent of graduates accepted job roles three months after graduation, and 10 percent started their own business.

 

8. Judge Business School, England

  • Tuition fees: US $70087
  • Percentage of international students: 51

Judge Business School is the MBA school of the grand University of Cambridge.

The 12-month MBA program at Judge Business School narrowly fell behind its biggest rival, Saïd Business School of Oxford University.

Judge’s one-year program scored very highly for the thought leadership and employability indicators, with 90 percent of graduates accepting jobs three months after graduating – noting an average salary of US $99753.

Cambridge highlights that students can tie their MBA program to their specific business goals through electives, a global consulting project, and concentrations.

They have 10 MBA concentrations, including Digital Transformation, Energy and Environment, Finance, and Marketing.

 

9. Imperial College Business School, England

  • Tuition fees: US $68910
  • Percentage of international students: 91

Imperial College Business School is under the Imperial College London. The 12-month London-based MBA program at Imperial scored very highly for thought leadership and employability indicators.

Students complete pre-study modules; they provide the essential knowledge and skills required for the program. It includes an accounting primer, a data analysis primer, and a finance primer.

Students can also attend the Imperial Innovation Challenge, a one-week learning lab finding and presenting a solution to a business problem, plus talks and workshops with industry experts.

Graduates note an average salary of US $82853, with 91 percent of graduates accepting jobs three months post-graduation.

 

10. SDA Bocconi, Italy

  • Tuition fees: US $66174
  • Percentage of international students: 74

SDA Bocconi School of Management is an excellent graduate business school, kicking off Europe’s top 10 is the 13-month MBA program.

This MBA program offers a perfect return on investment, sharing the top spot in the European top 10 with HEC Paris. Students can enjoy a salary up to 127 percent on average in post-graduation careers.

For nine months, program students take core classes and concentrations, with up to another six months. They can choose from individual internships, entrepreneurial projects, or group business labs to put their learning into practice.

Ninety percent of graduates accepted job offers within three months of graduating, with eight percent starting their own business three months post-graduation.
