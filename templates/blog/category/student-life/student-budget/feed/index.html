Australia, Cost of Studying Abroad for Indian Students

The quality of education, diverse culture, and beautiful landscapes make Australia an attractive and worthwhile choice for international students.

In 2023, India was Australia’s second-largest source country for international student enrollments, with over 95,000 Indian students pursuing their studies across various universities and institutions.

Although the cost of living in Australia tends to be higher, the overall expenses for studying in Australia remain more budget-friendly compared to the USA, UK, and Canada.

These are some estimations about the cost of studying in Australia for Indian students:

Expense Average Cost in Rupees
Tuition Fee 1,000,000 per annum
Accommodation 24,500 to 60,500 per month
Food 27,500 to 55,500 per month
Miscellaneous 20,000 to 30,000 per month
Total 155,500 to 229,500 per month

 

Pre-arrival costs, including airfare, exams, college applications, and visa charges, are approximately 162,000 rupees.

To ease study costs, international students can explore various scholarships like the following:

  • ANU Chancellor’s International Scholarship,
  • Gates Cambridge Scholarships,
  • UNSW’s Future of Change Scholarship,
  • and UWA’s Global Excellence Scholarship.

The Australian student visa allows part-time work, offering students valuable experience, though income for amounts exceeding 1,000,000 rupees is taxable.

 

Canada, Studying Abroad Expenses for Indian Students

In 2023, India was Canada’s most significant source country for international student enrollments, with over 226,000 Indian students pursuing their studies across various institutions.

A significant portion of the overall cost of studying in Canada is attributed to tuition fees at Canadian universities.

The specific amount of tuition varies based on the type of qualification and the institution an applicant selects for their studies in Canada.

These are some estimations about the cost of studying in Canada for Indian students:

Expense Average Cost in Rupees
Tuition Fee 1,900,000 per annum
Accommodation 23,500 to 45,000 per month
Food 18,000 to 24,500 per month
Miscellaneous 20,000 to 30,000 per month
Total 219,500 to 257,500 per month

 

Beyond tuition fees, expenses fluctuate based on the institution, chosen program, and individual lifestyle.

Pre-arrival costs, including tests, registration fees, and flight tickets, should be considered, along with purchasing a Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) and study permit expenses.

When selecting a college, students can estimate tuition fees, witnessing a 4.9% increase in the average. With varying costs across provinces, understanding the province-based tuition fees aids in making informed choices.

Germany, Cost of Studying Abroad for Indian Students

In 2023, India was Germany’s third-largest source country for international student enrollments, with over 35,000 Indian students pursuing their studies across various universities and institutions.

In Germany, many government universities provide education for free, no matter where you’re from. It makes it one of the most affordable places to study in Europe.

But, there might be some fees when you apply, and if you’re going for a Master’s degree without finishing your bachelor’s in Germany, you might have to pay tuition fees.

These are some estimations about the cost of studying abroad for indian students:

Expense Average Cost in Rupees
Tuition Fee 250,000 per annum
Accommodation 21,000 to 64,000 per month
Food 4,500 to 12,500 per month
Transportation 2,500 to 7,500 per month
Miscellaneous 18,000 to 27,000 per month
Total 67,000 to 132,000 per month

 

A government plan implemented in 2014 abolished tuition fees for all students, including international ones.

While a semester contribution fee of approximately 30,000 Euros covers student union, public transport, and administrative costs, private universities may charge around 3,000,000 rupees annually.

Course fees vary, with vocational or diploma courses bachelor’s, and master’s degrees costing around 30,000 rupees per year. Pursuing an MBA in Germany is cost-effective, especially at state-funded universities with no tuition fees.

UK, Studying Abroad Expenses for Indian Students

The UK has a rich tradition of embracing students from all over the world, providing Indian students with an academic and living experience that is unparalleled globally.

It is made possible by a cost of living that is within reach, an inclusive and diverse culture, and an environment that is safe and conducive to students’ needs.

These are some estimations about the cost of studying in the UK for Indian students:

Expense Average Cost in Rupees
Tuition Fee 970,000 per annum
Accommodation 43,000 to 60,000 per month
Household Bills 3,500 to 4,500 per month
Food 8,500 to 17,500 per month
Transportation 2,500 to 3,500 per month
Miscellaneous 17,000 to 26,000 per month
Total 155,500 to 192,500 per month

 

In the UK, the cost of education primarily hinges on tuition fees, a significant element of the overall expenditure. Among prominent private universities, those in England often charge around 940,000 rupees for various courses.

In Buckingham, MBA or MIM courses typically range from 1,400,000 to 1,600,000 rupees, while 3-year BBA courses may cost around 1,740,000 rupees.

London-based universities may have fees from 1,850,000 rupees for B.Sc courses to 2,500,000 rupees for MIM courses.

For public universities, prestigious institutions like Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial College London, London School of Economics and Political Science, and University College London have average tuition fees ranging from 1,150,000 to 1,850,000 rupees annually.

US, Cost of Studying Abroad for Indian Students

Substantial tuition fees primarily dominate the cost of education in the USA. Nevertheless, these fees fluctuate depending on the selected course, degree program, and university.

While tuition is a considerable aspect, financial aid, and scholarship opportunities can help alleviate the expenses for students pursuing education in the United States.

These are some estimations about cost of studying abroad for indian students:

Expense Average Cost in Rupees
Tuition Fee 4,000,000 per annum
Accommodation 65,000 to 75,000 per month
Food 8,000 to 11,000 per month
Miscellaneous 30,000 to 50,000 per month
Total 433,000 to 466,000 per month

 

Despite its high living expenses, the USA is a favored destination for Indian engineering students. Engineering degrees in the USA range from 3,000,000 to 4,000,000 rupees per year.

Notable universities include MIT, Harvard, Caltech, and Stanford, with annual fees varying. Popular engineering specializations include Software, Petroleum, Civil, Environmental, Mechanical, and Chemical Engineering.

English language exams like TOEFL or IELTS, costing around 14,000 to 15,000 rupees, are mandatory. Application fees range from 5,500 to 7,000 rupees, and sending TOEFL scores costs approximately 1,500.

The student visa application costs around 13,000 rupees, and airfare varies from 40,000 to 70,000. USA universities also offer scholarships for tuition, accommodation, travel, and health insurance.

Step 1: Eligibility Criteria for the Loan

If you need a loan, the first step is to check whether you are eligible to apply for education loans for study abroad or not.

The following are usual criteria which Indian banks have for checking the eligibility of the applicants:

  • The applicant must be an official citizen of India;
  • They must have an excellent academic background;
  • They should be at least 18, or the parents will have to take the loan;
  • The applicant must have secured admission to recognized foreign universities;
  • The course must be a professional or technical one because banks give preference to job-oriented classes.

 

Step 2: Required Documents of Loans for Study Abroad

In the second step, the applicant must know the required documents to apply for a study loan. The followings are necessary for applying for an education loan:

  • Filling the application form,
  • Photos in passport size of the applicant and the co-applicant,
  • Identification document of the applicant and the co-applicant. It may be a PAN card, driving license, or Passport,
  • Resident proof for the applicant and the co-applicant,
  • Educational documents from home school or university,
  • Score report and the certificates of the applicant,
  • Score report of TOEFL, IELTS, GRE, GMAT, etc. whichever is applicable,
  • Admission letter from the university or college,
  • Bank statement of last six months for the co-applicant,
  • Documents proving income of the co-applicant.

 

In the case of collateral, it can be a house or non-agriculture land; the following documents are required:

  • Building plan,
  • Property deed,
  • NOC for a mortgage.

 

Apart from the documents mentioned above, aspirants also need other technical documents to avail of a student loan, which includes:

  • Title deed and all the pages should be attached,
  • Copy of approved layout plan and permissions case-specific,
  • Revenue document, Khata or Patta,
  • BDA allotment or non-allotment letter,
  • Non-Encumbrance certificate,
  • Possession Certificate,
  • Latest Property tax receipt,
  • Copy of Prior sale deeds,
  • Conversion certificate,
  • Urban clearance certificate.

 

Moreover, aspirants should have legal documents for getting an education loan:

  • Sale, partition deed in favor of the customer, It should be a minimum of 13 years,
  • Khata Certificate and its extract in the name of the current owner,
  • Encumbrance certificate, Minimum 13 years reflecting all sale transactions,
  • Latest Property tax receipt.

 

Step 3: The Step-by-Step Process

The entire loan process is time-consuming, from loan application to approval and disbursement, so it is always suggested to apply for a loan early. You may follow these steps:

  1. Firstly, check the banks recognize the course in which you are going to study or not.
  2. Secondly, see how much money you need and how much you can arrange on your own.
  3. Then compare the student loan provided by different banks for studying abroad and go for the one which suits your needs.
  4. After finalizing the loan amount, fill out the loan application form and go to the bank.
  5. As soon as your loan is approved, the bank will issue a document that includes various loan elements.
  6. After signing the loan documents, the bank will disburse the amount in installments or as asked by the university.

7 Things to Remember When Taking Education Loans for Study Abroad

 When applying for education loans for study abroad as a student from India, there are some critical points to follow to accomplish your goal.

These are seven things shared by Indian students who already got their loans:

 

1. Moratorium Period

It is when the borrower need not make any repayment to the bank. This period varies from bank to bank and could last up to some time after completing the course.

 

2. The Margin of Loans for Study Abroad

Usually, banks do not provide the total amount needed to fund education. Most public banks issue 90% of the total amount, and the remaining 10% has to be arranged by the applicant.

 

3. Effect of Exchange Rate

Always calculate the amount you will receive at the time of disbursement, as any change in the rate may affect the applicant’s amount.

 

4. Collateral Rules of Loans for Study Abroad

Whether movable or immovable, any property can be offered to a bank as a security for a loan is collateral. Most Indian banks demand collateral as security to reduce the risk involved in paying off the credit.

Any liquid assets like bonds, FD, shares, or immovable property like the house, commercial property, or just land can be classified as collateral.

One crucial point to remember is that banks don’t accept agricultural land as collateral.

Bonds, deposits, shares, mutual fund units, gold, debentures, bank deposits, life insurance policy, and government securities, are accepted as collateral.

 

5. Guarantor Responsibilities

Becoming a loan guarantor is a great responsibility as the guarantor will have a legal obligation to pay if the borrower doesn’t. If the loan amount surpasses INR 4 lakhs, the bank may ask for a guarantee from a third party.

If the borrower cannot repay the loan amount irrespective of the circumstances, in that case, the guarantor holds the liability to reimburse the entire amount to banks.

 

6. Read the Agreement Carefully

It is one of the most important aspects to look after while taking a loan. It’s always best to go through the terms and conditions of the loan carefully and then move ahead with it.

We stress it upon one factor, as you should always be transparent with all the requirements mentioned in the loan agreement. If you have any uncertainties, consult the experts apart from the bank representative.

 

7. The Maximum Loan Amount

The banks set no limit for offering education loans to students abroad. It depends on the bank to bank.

The majority of the banks provide loans maximum up to INR 20 to 30 lakhs. Also, if the loan is more than INR 20 lakhs, then the interest rate increases respectively.
